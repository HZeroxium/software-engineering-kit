# Governance, Privacy, Compliance, and Risk

## Purpose

Governance is the discipline of making ownership, policy, accountability, and risk decisions explicit.

## Important Rule

Do not claim compliance unless evidence is provided. Do not invent legal obligations. Instead, identify likely risk areas and required owner/legal/security/privacy review.

## Governance Areas

- Service ownership.
- Data ownership.
- Architecture decisions.
- Policy enforcement.
- Privacy review.
- Security review.
- Vendor review.
- Cost ownership.
- Auditability.
- Risk acceptance.

## Privacy Areas

- Data minimization.
- Purpose limitation.
- Sensitive data classification.
- Retention and deletion.
- Access control.
- External sharing.
- Logging and telemetry.
- Model/provider data transfer.

## Compliance Risk Questions

- What data is processed?
- Who owns the data?
- What policy applies?
- What evidence is required?
- Who approves exceptions?
- How is compliance tested or audited?

## Failure Modes

- Compliance handled after release.
- No clear data owner.
- Sensitive data sent to model unnecessarily.
- Retention/deletion ignored.
- Audit trail missing.
- Vendor risk not reviewed.
